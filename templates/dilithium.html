CRYSTALS Dilithuim

Dilithium is a strongly EUF-CMA secure digital signature algorithm also designed to be secure against both classical and quantum computing attacks. Its use as a digital signature algorithm is to verify the authenticity and integrity of a message, software, or digital document. This ensures that the signature can only be generated by someone with access to the private key and cannot be unknowingly tampered after that. The message it uses to sign and verify communications is 59 bytes, allowing for fast and efficient security.

Dilithium has 3 different base parameter sets: Dilithium2, Dilithium3, and Dilithium5 which refer to increasing key sizes and higher levels of security of each implementation. There is also an additional variant for these parameter sets, Dilithium-AES, that uses AES-256 in counter mode instead of SHAKE to expand the matrix and the masking vectors, and to sample the secret polynomials. This AES-256 utilization may include its use for random number generation, adding additional layers of security to messages, or securing intermediate values in cryptographic processes. This adds the parameter sets of Dilithium2-AES, Dilithium3-AES, and Dilithium5-AES.
